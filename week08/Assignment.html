#same for the sex
dev$sex <- factor(unlist(dev$sex))

# The top most significant motifs

For each analysis, we - report , - plot a heatmap of the normalized accessibility scores across the samples for those motifs

Interpreting the results.

The heatmaps show the normalized accessibility scores of the top significant motifs across samples. Higher scores are linked to higher accessibility of the motifs in the samples.

The FSS vs control: we can see that some motifs have higher accessibility in the FSS samples, while others have higher accessibility in the control samples. Therefore, one could conclude that some motifs are more active and others are repressed in response to stress.

The male vs female: We can see that some motifs have higher accessibility in the female samples. This suggests that some motifs may be sex-specific, or that the accessibility of some motifs may be regulated by sex hormones.
